## Material choices for Antwerp projects

| Material | Best for | Avoid when |
| :-- | :-- | :-- |
| **PLA Matte** | models, displays, props, gifts, presentation pieces | heat, high load or long outdoor exposure |
| **PETG** | functional parts, light outdoor use, holders and covers | maximum surface detail matters more than toughness |
| **TPU** | grips, bumpers, dampers, protective covers and flexible parts | the part needs to stay rigid |
| **ASA/ABS or Nylon** | technical parts, higher temperature and heavier use | only after a material check |

If you are unsure, send the use case. A fair booth component is not the same as a warehouse scanner clip or a museum plinth part.

## FAQ about 3D printing in Antwerp

**Do you deliver to all Antwerp districts?**  
Yes. We ship to Antwerp, Berchem, Borgerhout, Deurne, Ekeren, Hoboken, Merksem, Wilrijk, Borsbeek and Berendrecht-Zandvliet-Lillo. Pickup in Herzele is possible by appointment.

**Can you make event materials or retail displays?**  
Yes. Examples include displays, logo objects, price tags, booth pieces, giveaways, table decoration and modular props. We consider finishing, transport and assembly.

**Is 3D printing useful for port or logistics work?**  
For many small tooling parts, guards, scanner mounts, clips and test setups, yes. For load, heat or safety-critical use, we review material, wall thickness and risk first.

**Can you review my file before I order?**  
Yes. Send STL or STEP with a short explanation. We check wall thickness, sizing, orientation and printability.

**What if I only have photos or a broken part?**  
Send photos to discuss feasibility. For reproduction or reverse engineering, the object usually needs to come in physically, especially when fit matters.
